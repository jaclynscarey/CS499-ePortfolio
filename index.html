Professional Self-Assessment

Completing my coursework throughout the Computer Science program at SNHU and developing the ePortfolio has helped me reflect on how far I've come both professionally and personally. I am currently employed as a Software Development Engineer at Adobe, where I have had the opportunity to work on impactful projects involving mobile application performance, automation, and cross-platform systems. However, my academic journey at SNHU has significantly deepened my understanding of core computer science concepts, and I feel more ready than ever to take on greater responsibilities and pursue future promotions within my organization. My Computer Science degree includes a concentration in Information Security, which further complements my focus on building secure, scalable systems.

This program has helped me shape my professional goals by reinforcing the importance of foundational knowledge, practical application, and lifelong learning. Through my coursework, I honed my abilities in secure coding practices, algorithm design, database implementation, and collaborative development. I've also learned how to assess trade-offs when making design choices and gained confidence in building scalable, maintainable applications.

Outside of technical growth, the program emphasized real-world collaboration, communication, and project documentation. Presenting my work through code reviews and written documentation taught me how to explain complex technical ideas to both technical and non-technical stakeholders. This skill has been particularly useful in my role at Adobe, where cross-functional collaboration is essential.

My experience also includes practicing Agile methodologies and Scrum practices, including earning my PSM I, Professional Scrum Master I, certification. These practices have enabled me to thrive in team-based environments where iteration, feedback, and flexibility are key.

From a technical perspective, my studies have strengthened my skills in data structures and algorithms through targeted assignments and enhancement projects. I explored time complexity, sorting techniques, and optimized data retrieval in my capstone project. I also gained hands-on experience in software engineering through full-stack development, including user authentication, secure data handling, and API integration. My database coursework and final enhancement showcased my ability to implement scalable and secure data storage using MongoDB.

Security was another recurring theme throughout the program. I practiced building secure systems by implementing OAuth, restricting access, validating user input, and learning about common vulnerabilities and mitigation techniques. This security-focused mindset has become part of my daily engineering practice.

The artifacts in my portfolio come together to form a cohesive picture of my capabilities. Each enhancement builds upon a single full-stack project, the Recipe Finder App, allowing me to demonstrate progress across three critical areas of computer science: software engineering, algorithms and data structures, and databases. In each phase, I identified gaps, implemented meaningful improvements, and documented my reasoning and outcomes.

Together, these enhancements demonstrate not only my ability to write effective code but also my understanding of user experience, performance optimization, and secure data handling. They illustrate my readiness for higher-level responsibilities and my ability to contribute value in real-world engineering environments.

This portfolio tells the story of a developer who combines hands-on skill with strategic thinking and continuous learning—qualities I will continue to apply as I advance in my career.
